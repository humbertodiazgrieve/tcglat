One Piece is the most successful manga and anime in Japanese history. With more than 500 million copies sold and a story that has lasted decades, it was only a matter of time before it received its own card game. One Piece Card Game arrived in 2022 and, since then, has become one of the best-selling TCGs in the world. In this article we review its history, launch, and evolution.

## The origin of One Piece

One Piece was created by Eiichiro Oda and began publication in 1997 in Weekly Shonen Jump magazine. The story follows Monkey D. Luffy and his crew of pirates in search of the legendary treasure known as One Piece, which would grant the title of Pirate King.

Over the years, One Piece has become a global cultural phenomenon. The anime, movies, video games, and merchandising products have kept the franchise at the peak of popularity. With such a fan base, the leap to card games was practically inevitable.

## The announcement of One Piece Card Game

Bandai announced One Piece Card Game in 2021, with an initial launch in Japan in 2022. The news generated enormous expectation, as for the first time fans would have an official TCG of their favorite series. The game design was intended to be accessible to new players but with enough depth to attract competitive players.

The first set, **Romance Dawn**, took its name from the initial arc of the manga and featured the series' most iconic characters. Cards of Luffy, Zoro, Nami, Sanji, and other members of the Straw Hat crew were the protagonists of the launch.

## Global launch and success

After success in Japan, One Piece Card Game expanded to international markets. The global launch was received with enthusiasm by One Piece fans and by the trading card game community in general. Products sold out quickly in many stores, and demand exceeded supply on several occasions.

The game attracts not only One Piece collectors but also TCG players looking for a new challenge. Its don mechanics, colors, and leaders offer an experience different from other popular card games.

## Expansions and arcs

Each One Piece Card Game expansion is based on arcs or themes from the series. Some of the most notable sets include:

- **Romance Dawn**: the inaugural set that introduced the game world.
- **Paramount War**: centered on the Marineford war.
- **Pillars of Strength**: with characters from the New World.
- **Kingdoms of Intrigue**: exploring political and intrigue arcs.
- **Wano**: based on the popular Wano Country arc.

Each expansion adds new characters, events, stages, and mechanics, keeping the game fresh and exciting.

## Impact on the TCG market

One Piece Card Game has shown that TCGs based on popular franchises can compete with the market giants. In Japan, it came to surpass established games in sales, consolidating itself as one of the best-selling TCGs in the country. Its success has also driven the organization of official tournaments, store events, and international competitions.

The One Piece TCG collectible market has also grown. Cards of popular characters, foil versions, and special editions reach high prices on the secondary market.

## Conclusion

The history of One Piece Card Game is the story of a franchise that has known how to expand without losing its essence. From the 1997 manga to the 2022 TCG, One Piece has demonstrated a unique ability to connect with generations of fans. The card game is not only a way to enjoy the series, but also a competitive and social experience that continues to grow.
