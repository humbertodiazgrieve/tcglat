The Pokémon universe transcends video games and trading cards to extend into the world of manga and comics. From the pages of the original manga by Satoshi Tajiri to the commercial adaptations that adorn magazines around the world, the visual universe of Pokémon has found an important home in the comic format. This exhaustive guide will take you through the main Pokémon manga series, their stories, characters, and where you can enjoy these works.

## The Original Pokémon Manga: Pokémon Special

The most emblematic and longest-running manga series is Pokémon Special, originally known as Pocket Monsters Special in Japan. This series was created by Hidenaga Katakami and later continued by Satoshi Yamamoto. First published in 1997 in CoroCoro Comic magazine, this series has continued for more than 25 years, adapting to each generation of Pokémon games.

The story of Pokémon Special follows Red, Green, and Blue, three young Trainers who begin their adventures in the Kanto region. Unlike the anime, which is aimed at a younger audience, the manga adopts a darker and more mature tone, exploring more complex themes and presenting more strategic battles. The characters face more difficult challenges and the consequences of their actions carry greater weight in the narrative.

The series has been licensed and translated into numerous languages, including English, Spanish, French, and many others. In some countries, the translations have been published both in tankōbon format (collected volumes) and in digital publications. The longevity of the series is extraordinary, with more than 50 volumes published covering the first seven generations of Pokémon.

## Other Important Manga Series

Besides Pokémon Special, there are numerous derivative manga series that expand the Pokémon universe in different directions. The most notable is probably Pokémon: The Electric Tale of Pikachu, a slice-of-life manga that follows the adventures of Ash Ketchum and his Pikachu in a lighter and more comedic tone. This series was originally published in Japan by Shogakukan and later licensed in English by Viz Media.

Pokémon Adventures: Let's Explore the Pokémon World! is another notable series that focuses on the travels of characters related to Gym Leaders and the Elite Four. This series offers a different perspective on the Pokémon world, focusing on secondary characters who would normally be antagonists or non-playable characters in the video games.

There are also numerous one-shots and short series based on specific aspects of the Pokémon universe. These include manga centered on specific creatures, such as the Mewtwo manga or stories about the legendaries. Other series have explored the worlds of spin-offs, such as Pokémon Mystery Dungeon or the Pico series games.

## Pokémon Comics in the West

The Western market has produced its own comic publications of Pokémon, although in smaller quantities than Japanese publications. The most notable is the series published by The Pokémon Company International in partnership with publishers like Dark Horse Comics and later with IDW Publishing. These publications include adaptations of existing stories and material originally created specifically for the Western market.

Dark Horse Comics published several Pokémon comic arcs in English, including stories that served as prequels to the animated movies and material that expanded the established lore. Although these publications were eventually discontinued, they remain valuable collector pieces for fans who managed to get them during their publication period.

IDW Publishing has continued the tradition of publishing Pokémon comics in English, with series that include adaptations of the original manga and new material. These publications have helped introduce the universe of Pokémon manga to Western audiences who otherwise would not have had access to Japanese publications.

## The Digital Format and New Platforms

The digital era has significantly transformed the way readers access Pokémon manga. Platforms like Crunchyroll Manga, Viz Media Shonen Jump, and other digital reading services offer access to many Pokémon manga series. These platforms allow readers to access original Japanese versions and translations in English and other languages with launches almost simultaneous with Japanese publications.

Kindle Unlimited and other e-book services also offer options for reading Pokémon manga in digital format. Although the digital reading experience is different from the physical format, it offers convenience and accessibility that attracts many modern readers. Digital versions are usually cheaper than physical ones, especially for complete series.

Manga reading apps on mobile devices have made it easier than ever to read Pokémon series while traveling. Readers can store dozens of volumes on their devices and access them at any time. This accessibility has contributed to a resurgence of interest in manga in general and Pokémon manga specifically.

## The Illustrations and the Art of the Mangaka

The art in Pokémon manga is diverse and has evolved significantly over the decades. The original artists established a style that was later refined and adapted by numerous successors. Each artist contributes their own distinctive touch while maintaining the visual essence that fans expect.

The designs of Pokémon in manga often differ slightly from their counterparts in the video games and anime. Manga artists have more creative freedom and can explore aspects of the designs that other media do not allow. This has resulted in unique interpretations of creatures that have become fan favorites.

The covers of Pokémon manga volumes are particularly admired by collectors. The full-cover illustrations, character designs, and action scenes have made these volumes desired objects for fans and collectors alike. Limited editions with alternate covers are especially coveted.

## Where to Find and Buy Pokémon Manga

For those interested in acquiring Pokémon manga physically, there are several options. Specialized manga and anime bookstores usually have selections of popular titles, including those of Pokémon. Online stores like Amazon, Book Depository, and specialized anime stores offer extensive selections with international shipping.

Anime and manga conventions are excellent places to find Pokémon manga, including rare items and out-of-print titles. Events like anime conventions in Peru offer the opportunity to find difficult-to-locate titles. Many independent vendors offer products at these events.

For readers who prefer not to buy, public and university libraries may have Pokémon manga collections available for loan. Some libraries have begun to expand their manga collections to meet the demand of their communities. Library lending offers a way to explore series without financial commitment.

## Collecting Pokémon Manga

Collecting Pokémon manga is a rewarding hobby for many fans. Complete volumes of series like Pokémon Special can be a significant addition to any collection. First editions, especially those with artist signatures, can reach significant prices in the collector market.

Special and limited editions are particularly sought after by collectors. These include versions with alternate covers, extra materials, and collector editions that were only available in Japan or in limited quantities. The collector manga market has grown significantly, with prices reflecting the demand for rare pieces.

Organizing a Pokémon manga collection can be a fun project. Volumes can be organized by series, by Pokémon generation, or simply by order of acquisition. Specially designed display cases for manga and comics can show off collections while protecting them from damage caused by light and handling.

## The Future of Pokémon Manga

Pokémon manga continues to evolve with the franchise. New generations of Pokémon inspire new stories and artists. Manga remains a popular medium for exploring the Pokémon universe in ways that video games and anime cannot match.

Collaborations between The Pokémon Company and manga studios continue to produce new content. Recent projects have explored aspects of the Pokémon universe that had not been covered before, maintaining the interest of long-time fans while attracting new readers.

The Pokémon manga fan community is active and dedicated. Online forums, social media groups, and conventions provide spaces to discuss series, share recommendations, and connect with other enthusiasts. This community helps keep interest in Pokémon manga alive and supports the continuation of new publications.

## Conclusion

Pokémon manga and comics offer a unique way to experience the universe of your favorite creatures. From the main Pokémon Special series to the numerous derivative series, there is content to satisfy the tastes of all types of readers. Whether you prefer the mature tone of the original manga or the lighter stories of the derivative series, the world of Pokémon manga has something to offer. Explore the numerous available options and discover new ways to enjoy the Pokémon universe through the pages of manga.
