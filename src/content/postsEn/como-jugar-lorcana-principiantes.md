Disney Lorcana has arrived in the trading card game world with a unique proposal: mixing the magic of Disney characters with deep, competitive mechanics. If you are a Disney fan and want to enter the universe of TCGs, Lorcana is an excellent entry point. In this guide we explain step by step how to play Disney Lorcana.

## What do you need to play?

To play Disney Lorcana you need a deck of at least 60 cards. Unlike other games, in Lorcana there is no minimum life total to reduce. The objective is to earn **20 lore points** before your opponent.

You also need an opponent with their own deck. The cards have spectacular illustrations of characters, places, and elements from Disney, so preparing the table is already a visual experience.

## Card types in Lorcana

### Characters

**Characters** are creature cards that represent heroes, villains, and iconic Disney figures. They have an ink cost, strength, willpower, and special abilities. Characters can perform three main actions:

- **Sing**: use a song to activate an effect.
- **Challenge**: face an opposing character to damage or banish it.
- **Explore**: gain lore to get closer to victory.

Each character enters the game **exhausted**, which means it cannot act on the same turn it is played, unless it has a special ability like **rush**.

### Action Cards

Action cards represent spells, events, or resources that resolve immediately. They are played from the hand, their effect resolves, and then they go to the discard pile. They are ideal for removing threats, drawing cards, or modifying the game state.

### Items

Items are cards that remain on the battlefield and grant continuous effects. They can empower your characters, make the opponent's plays more difficult, or generate value throughout the game. They are key in many control and value strategies.

### Songs

Songs are a special type of card that your characters can sing. To sing a song, you need a character on the field with the appropriate ability. Singing allows you to activate powerful effects without spending additional ink, making them a very efficient resource.

## The ink system

**Ink** is the resource of Lorcana. It works similarly to mana in other games, but with a particular mechanic: at the start of each turn, you can **place one card face down** from your hand as ink. Those cards are removed from play to generate resources, but they can never be used as normal cards again.

**Important rules:**
- Each turn you can place one card as ink.
- Only cards without ink in their cost can be used as ink (normally indicated by an icon).
- Accumulated ink is spent to play cards of the corresponding cost.

This means you must decide which cards to sacrifice as ink and which to keep. Resource management is one of the most important skills in Lorcana.

## Lorcana colors

Lorcana has six colors, each with its own identity and strategy:

- **Amber**: focus on support, healing, and supporting characters.
- **Amethyst**: control, manipulation, and card effects.
- **Emerald**: aggression, evasion, and fast combos.
- **Ruby**: direct damage, aggression, and challenges.
- **Sapphire**: card advantage, resource control, and lore.
- **Steel**: defense, resilience, and durable characters.

Most decks combine two colors to take advantage of synergies. For example, Amber-Amethyst is popular for its control and support capabilities, while Ruby-Emerald is aggressive and direct.

## How to win a game

The objective is to be the first player to reach **20 lore points**. To gain lore, your characters must explore. However, exploring leaves your characters vulnerable to the opponent's challenges. You must decide when to risk going for lore and when to defend your board.

You can also win if your opponent runs out of cards in their deck and must draw, although this situation is less common.

## Building your first deck

If you are starting out, the easiest option is to buy an official **starter deck**. These decks come ready to play and are an excellent introduction to the mechanics.

When building your own deck, consider:

- Keep at least 60 cards.
- Include a balance of characters, actions, and items.
- Choose two colors that synergize with your strategy.
- Include ways to generate lore and defend it.
- Do not exceed 4 copies of the same card.

## Tips for beginners

- **Plan your ink**: deciding which cards to convert into ink is key.
- **Don't explore blindly**: make sure your characters can survive the opponent's challenge.
- **Read the abilities**: many cards have subtle effects that change the game.
- **Balance aggression and defense**: going only for lore can leave you exposed.
- **Practice with a starter deck**: learn the mechanics before building from scratch.
