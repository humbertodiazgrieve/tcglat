The first generation of Pokémon marked the birth of a franchise that would become a global cultural phenomenon. Originally released in 1996 for Game Boy, these 151 Pokémon have left an indelible mark on the history of video games and entertainment. In this complete guide, we explore why the first-generation Pokémon remain the most valued by collectors and fans around the world.

## The 151 Originals: A Legendary List

When Pokémon Red and Green were released in Japan in 1996, no one anticipated that they would become a worldwide phenomenon. Director Satoshi Tajiri created these games inspired by his own childhood collecting insects, and this passion is reflected in each of the 151 original Pokémon. From the iconic Pikachu to the mysterious Mew, each creature was designed with a unique personality that resonated with millions of players.

The first generation introduced concepts that remain fundamental to the saga to this day. Elemental types, evolutions, special attacks, and the concept of capturing and training creatures were established in these original games. Although many mechanics have evolved, the foundations of the franchise were built with these 151 original designs that continue to inspire new generations of players.

The impact of these Pokémon transcends video games. Merchandising, trading cards, anime, movies, and practically every aspect of entertainment has been affected by these characters. Charizard, Blastoise, Venusaur, Pikachu, and many others have become globally recognizable icons, appearing in advertising campaigns, fashion products, and practically every imaginable form of media.

## The Most Iconic Kanto Pokémon

### The Kanto Starters

Every Pokémon journey begins with the choice of your first partner. In Kanto, players have the option of choosing between Bulbasaur, Charmander, and Squirtle, three Pokémon that represent the Grass, Fire, and Water types respectively. This decision has been the subject of debate among fans for decades, and each one has its own dedicated followers.

Bulbasaur, the Seed Pokémon, evolves into Venusaur, an imposing Grass-type Pokémon with a flower on its back. Its unique design and its Grass type make it especially useful against Rock and Water-type Gym Leaders. Charmander, the Fire lizard, transforms into Charmeleon and finally Charizard, one of the most popular Pokémon in the entire saga. Its Fire and Flying-type dragon design has captivated fans since its initial appearance.

Squirtle, the tiny turtle Water-type, evolves into Wartortle and Blastoise, the turtle Pokémon with water cannons integrated into its shell. Blastoise has been a pillar in competitive play since the first games, and its presence in the anime made it one of the most recognized starters. The choice between these three defines the initial experience of the game and creates lasting emotional connections with players.

### The Legendaries of the First Generation

The legendaries of the first generation represent some of the most powerful and mysterious Pokémon in the entire universe. Moltres, Zapdos, and Articuno form the trio of legendary birds, each representing the Fire, Electric, and Ice types respectively. These Pokémon were introduced as extremely rare creatures that could be caught in specific locations in the Kanto world.

Mewtwo, scientifically created from Mew's DNA, is considered one of the most powerful Pokémon ever created. Its tragic story as a laboratory creature makes it one of the most memorable antagonists in the anime and games. On the other hand, Mew, the ancestor Pokémon, is a living enigma that according to Pokémon lore is the ancestor of all creatures. Its rarity and connection to the origin of life make it one of the most coveted Pokémon.

Mew and Mewtwo are not the only legendaries of this generation. The legendary trios would become a tradition of the saga, but the originals established the standard. The search for these Pokémon in the original games required completing specific tasks and exploring hidden corners of the map, creating memorable experiences for players of the era.

## Nostalgic Value and Collecting

The value of first-generation Pokémon in the collecting market is extraordinary. First-edition Pokémon TCG cards, especially the holographic cards of legendaries and starters, can reach prices of thousands of dollars. The rarer variants, such as event promotional cards or limited editions, are extremely coveted by collectors around the world.

Beyond monetary value, these Pokémon have incalculable nostalgic value. For millions of adults who played these games in their childhood, the Kanto Pokémon represent a connection to precious memories of their youth. This emotional connection is what keeps interest in the first generation alive, even as the saga evolves and new generations are introduced.

The market for reprints and remasters has shown that demand for first-generation content remains strong. Remakes like Pokémon FireRed and LeafGreen, as well as remasters on Nintendo Switch, have allowed new generations to experience the magic of Kanto. However, the original games have a special status among collectors and hardcore fans.

## Impact on the Pokémon TCG

The Pokémon Trading Card Game began with the first generation, and cards from this era remain some of the most valuable in the game. Original cards of Charizard, Blastoise, and Venusaur in their holographic forms are considered treasures by collectors. The vintage card market has experienced exponential growth in recent years, with prices continuing to rise.

The reprints of classic cards keep the spirit of the first generation accessible to new players. However, the original cards of the Base Set have a special status. The rarity of these cards, combined with their historical importance, makes them potential investments as much as cherished collectibles.

For competitive players, some first-generation cards remain relevant in specific formats. Although the meta has evolved significantly, the legacy of the game continues to honor the original Pokémon. The battles of these cards in tournaments remind players of the rich history of the game and keep alive the connection with the first days of the TCG.

## The First Generation in Anime and Media

The Pokémon anime turned many of these characters into international stars. Ash Ketchum, the human protagonist, began his journey in Pallet Town alongside Pikachu, a Pokémon that became the official symbol of the franchise. The relationship between Ash and Pikachu has been the heart of the anime for more than 25 years, entertaining audiences of all ages.

Other characters from the anime, such as Misty and Brock, accompanied Ash on his first adventures through Kanto. Gym Leaders like Giovanni, Surge, and Sabrina also appeared as antagonists or challenges that Ash had to overcome. This expansion of the Kanto universe beyond the games created a richer narrative and memorable characters that transcended the video game format.

The Pokémon movies, particularly those based on legendaries like Mewtwo Strikes Back, were box office successes around the world. These movies expanded the mythology of the Pokémon and created stories that remain in the collective memory of fans. The impact of the anime on the popularity of Pokémon cannot be underestimated, as it introduced the franchise to audiences who perhaps would never have played the video games.

## Conclusion

The first-generation Pokémon represent much more than simple virtual creatures. They are the fundamental pillars of a franchise that has lasted more than 25 years, creating indelible memories for multiple generations of fans. Whether for their nostalgic value, their historical importance, or simply their timeless design, the 151 original Kanto Pokémon remain the most beloved and valued in the Pokémon universe. For any fan or collector, these classic Pokémon will never lose their special value.
