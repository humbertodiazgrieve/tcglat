The Kanto region is the place where everything began for millions of Pokémon Trainers around the world. Introduced in the legendary Pokémon Red and Green in 1996, Kanto has remained one of the most iconic environments in video game history. This complete guide will take you through every aspect of this legendary region, from its cities and gyms to the 151 Pokémon that call this land home.

## History and Origin of the Kanto Region

The Kanto region was created by Satoshi Tajiri and the Game Freak team as the setting for the first Pokémon games. The name "Kanto" is based on the Kanto region in Japan, which includes Tokyo and its surroundings. This area was chosen by Tajiri due to his familiarity with it and because it offered a variety of landscapes that allowed for a diverse and attractive gameplay experience. The geography of Kanto, with its mix of developed urban areas and pristine nature, reflects the reality of the Japanese region that inspired it.

The development of Kanto was innovative for its time. Unlike other role-playing games of the era, Kanto presented a vast open world where players could explore freely after completing the initial challenges. This freedom of exploration, combined with the mechanic of capturing and training Pokémon, created an experience that resonated deeply with players. The structure of the Kanto world, with routes connecting cities and other locations, established the pattern that later game regions would follow.

The cultural importance of Kanto cannot be underestimated. This region has been the setting for some of the most memorable moments in video game history. From choosing the starter Pokémon to the first time players faced the Pokémon League, every experience in Kanto has been etched into the memory of millions of fans. This nostalgia has been leveraged by Nintendo and The Pokémon Company on multiple occasions, with remakes and revisions that keep Kanto relevant in current generations.

## The Cities and Towns of Kanto

Kanto contains a total of 20 explorable locations, including cities, towns, routes, caves, special buildings, and other areas. Each of these locations has its own character and offers different gameplay experiences. From the small Pallet Town where your adventure begins, to the imposing Pokémon League where your journey ends, every place in Kanto has something special to offer.

### Pallet Town: The Beginning of Everything

Pallet Town is the location where all Trainers begin their journey. This small town, composed of just a few houses, is home to Professor Oak, the renowned Pokémon researcher who will give you your first Pokémon. Professor Oak's house also serves as one of the first save points of the game, where players can register their Trainer information.

The Pallet Town Science Museum offers an educational view of the Pokémon world. Although it is an optional area, the museum contains valuable information about the different types of Pokémon and their natural habitats. For players looking to better understand the game mechanics, a visit to the museum can be particularly useful.

### Viridian City: The First Goal

Viridian City is the first major city that players encounter on their journey. Here is the first Kanto Gym, led by Brock, the Rock-type Gym Leader. This gym represents the first real challenge for new Trainers, and overcoming it is a rite of passage that marks the transition from novice to true Pokémon Trainer.

Viridian City also offers the opportunity to visit the Pokémon Center, where players can heal their Pokémon for free. This free healing mechanic was revolutionary at the time and remains a fundamental aspect of the series. In addition, the city's item store allows players to equip themselves with essential items for their journey.

### The Remaining Cities of Kanto

Cerulean City, home of Gym Leader Lt. Surge, is known for its military base and military theme. The city presents an interesting contrast with the rest of the region, offering a unique atmosphere that distinguishes it from other locations. The Cerulean City Gym is particularly memorable for many players due to its design and the challenge it presents.

Saffron City, located on the coast, is home to Gym Leader Sabrina, a Psychic-type master. This city offers a more relaxed atmosphere compared to the previous ones, and its proximity to the sea makes it a popular location for merchants and sailors. The abandoned mansion on the outskirts of the city is another point of interest for players looking for unique Pokémon.

Lavender Town, although technically a town rather than a city, deserves special mention for being home to the Poké Community Center and the Pokémon Tower. This tower, which functions as a cemetery for Pokémon, has a unique atmosphere that differentiates it from all other locations in Kanto. The presence of mysteries and legends around the tower makes it one of the most memorable places in the region.

## The Gyms of Kanto: A Test of Skill

Kanto contains eight Pokémon Gyms, each led by a Gym Leader specialized in a specific type of Pokémon. Defeating these leaders and obtaining the eight Kanto badges is the main requirement to face the Pokémon League and become Champion. Each Gym presents a unique challenge that tests different player skills.

### Recommended Gym Order

The standard order of the gyms in Kanto is: Rock (Pewter City), Water (Cerulean City), Electric (Vermilion City), Grass (Celadon City), Psychic (Saffron City), Fighting (Cinnabar Island), Poison (Fuchsia City), and Ground (Viridian City). This order is not mandatory, and players can choose to face the gyms in any sequence, although some are significantly more difficult without adequate preparation.

Gym Leader Brock, with his Rock-type Pokémon, is the first challenge faced by new players. His Onix and later his Poliwrath can be difficult to defeat for teams without preparation. The key to defeating Brock is to have Water- or Grass-type Pokémon that can exploit the Rock type's weaknesses.

Lt. Surge in Cerulean City presents a different challenge. His team of Electric-type Pokémon can deal massive damage to teams that depend on Flying- or Water-type Pokémon. The optimal strategy against this gym involves using Ground-type Pokémon for immunity to Electric attacks and effective counterattacks.

## The Routes of Kanto: Adventure and Discovery

The routes connecting the cities of Kanto are much more than simple paths. These routes contain wild Pokémon that players can find and capture, plus hidden items and secondary characters. Exploring the routes is essential for building a strong team and completing the Pokédex.

Route 1, which connects Pallet Town with Viridian City, is the first combat area for most players. The wild Pokémon found here, such as Pidgey and Rattata, are perfect for the initial level of the game. Although these Pokémon are common, some players find it useful to catch them for additional options on their team.

The more advanced routes, such as Route 23 and Route 24, present significantly greater challenges. These routes contain higher-level Pokémon and may require more prepared teams to navigate safely. The route leading to the Pokémon League is particularly difficult, with formidable Pokémon Trainers that test players' skills.

## The 151 Pokémon of Kanto

The Kanto region is home to the 151 original Pokémon, which were the only Pokémon that existed until the release of the second generation. This collection includes some of the most popular Pokémon in the entire series, including Pikachu, Charizard, Mew, and many others.

### The Kanto Starters

The three Kanto starters, Bulbasaur, Charmander, and Squirtle, represent the initial options that players can choose at the beginning of their adventure. Each one evolves into a Stage 2 Pokémon with unique characteristics that distinguish them. The final evolutions, Venusaur, Charizard, and Blastoise, remain some of the most iconic Pokémon in the entire series.

Charizard, the evolution of Charmander, has reached an almost legendary status as one of the most popular Pokémon of all time. Its Fire dragon design, combined with its battle utility, has made it a fan favorite for decades. In competitive play, Charizard has been a popular choice in multiple formats over the years.

Blastoise, the final evolution of Squirtle, is another starter that has left an indelible mark on the franchise. Its water cannons integrated into its shell make it instantly recognizable. In the anime, Misty's Blastoise became one of the most memorable companions of the protagonist.

### The Legendaries of Kanto

The legendaries of Kanto include the three legendary birds, Articuno, Zapdos, and Moltres, plus Mewtwo and Mew. These creatures represent some of the most powerful and mysterious Pokémon of the region. Their extraordinarily high base stats make them formidable options for any team.

Mewtwo, created from Mew's DNA, is frequently cited as one of the most powerful Pokémon in the universe. Its appearance in the movie "Pokémon: Mewtwo Strikes Back" catapulted it to international fame. In the games, Mewtwo has consistently been one of the most popular choices for competitive teams.

Mew, considered the ancestor of all Pokémon, is another legendary that has fascinated fans since the first games. Its extreme rarity in the original games, which required special events to obtain it, made it one of the most coveted Pokémon. The legend of Mew as the origin of all life adds depth to its already significant status and importance.

## The Pokémon League of Kanto

The Kanto Pokémon League represents the final challenge that Trainers must overcome to become Champions. This institution is composed of the Elite Four, the four most powerful Trainers in the region, and the Champion, who occupies the highest position. Defeating these Trainers requires well-prepared teams and solid strategies.

The members of the Kanto Elite Four are Lorelei (Ice), Bruno (Fighting), Agatha (Poison), and Lance (Dragon). Each one has a team specialized in their type, presenting unique challenges that require different strategies to overcome. The team composition of the Elite Four has been replicated and referenced on numerous occasions throughout the series.

The Champion of Kanto, in the original games, is Blue. After losing to the player in the League, Blue becomes the Champion and waits for an opportunity for a rematch. This narrative was expanded in the remakes, where the story of the League develops with greater depth.

## Modern Updates of Kanto

The Kanto region has been updated and revisited on multiple occasions over the years. The remakes of Pokémon FireRed and LeafGreen for Game Boy Advance maintained the essence of the region while adding graphical improvements and updated mechanics. These games allowed a new generation to experience the magic of Kanto.

More recently, Pokémon Let's Go Pikachu and Let's Go Eevee brought Kanto to Nintendo Switch with improved graphics and simplified mechanics. These games served as a bridge between the classic games and the modern main series, maintaining nostalgia while offering an experience adapted to modern expectations.

The Pokémon HOME game, which allows players to transfer their Pokémon between modern games, includes support for Kanto Pokémon. This integration ensures that Pokémon caught in any game of the region can be transported to the most recent experiences, maintaining the relevance of these Pokémon in the current meta.

## Conclusion

The Kanto region represents much more than the setting of the first Pokémon games. It is a place that has captured the imagination of millions of players for more than 25 years. From its memorable cities to its legendary Pokémon, every aspect of Kanto has been designed to create immersive and memorable experiences. Whether you are visiting the region for the first time or returning to relive nostalgic memories, Kanto always has something special to offer. The land of the 151 original Pokémon remains a fundamental destination for any Pokémon Trainer.
